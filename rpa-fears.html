Your fears. Said out loud.
Pulled from the RPA team's pre-cohort survey. Anonymized, lightly edited for length.
"Time spent, not knowing the right AI tools."
"Making sure I learn how to use AI in a practical and sustainable way that genuinely improves my daily work rather than adding more complexity."
"I want to focus on solutions that save time, improve processes, and can be realistically integrated into the tools and workflows I already use."

What we heard, in three themes

Tool overwhelm
Too many AI tools, not enough signal on which ones are actually worth your time. Fear of burning hours evaluating before any real work gets done.
Practical over flashy
Worry that the cohort produces clever demos but nothing that survives Monday morning. The bar is sustainable change, not novelty.
Integration, not islands
Concern about adding another disconnected tool to a stack that already has too many. Whatever gets built has to plug into the systems the team uses now.
These are the fears we're building against. Every session is designed to take one of them off the table.
