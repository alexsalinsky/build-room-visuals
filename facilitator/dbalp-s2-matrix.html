Companion to: s2-facilitation-brief.md (long-form per-person briefs).
Use: show this live in S2 after the Starter Pack and Build Plan Loop teachings. Scroll through it as a group. Each person sees their row. Anyone can flag "yes that's it" or "no, not that" or "actually it's more like X." Modify in real time.

Columns:
- Project — the thing they're building, in 5-8 words
- Their own words — verbatim from S1 transcript or canvas
- Step 1 (you) — what they do themselves first
- Step 2 (Claude) — what they ask Claude to do
- Step 3 (score) — what they compare and rate
- Step 4 (refine) — what they correct
- Win moment — the pass/fail you celebrate at share-out
- Principle — the teaching their project surfaces
- Taste of S4 — what their S2 artifact previews about demo day
- Check-in line — what to say to them at share-out

How to run this in the room

  1. After the Build Plan Loop teaching, share-screen this matrix.
  2. Walk row by row. ~60 sec each. "Here's what I heard you say in S1. Here's the loop we'd walk during the build block. Here's the principle. Here's what'd feel like a win. Here's where it lands at demo day."
  3. After each row, ask: "Does that land? Anything off?" Take their correction live. Update the cell on the fly.
  4. After Bryce + the 8: lead into the 35-min build block with the loop visual back on screen. They each work their own row.
  5. At share-out (Stage 5): each person shows their win and names their principle. Matrix becomes the score card — they tick their own row.

Things to flag while reviewing

  • Bryce's row is intentionally different. Don't apologize for it — name it. "Your role in S2 is multiplier. We need you in seven places, not one."
  • Kate's principle ("meet the platform where it is") is a teaching for the whole room — call it out for everyone, not just Kate. It generalizes.
  • Bryce's row references his 400k reporter scrape (he said "one week left" on June 9, so should be done by S3 on June 23). Confirm with him at S2 that this is still on track — Kandice's S4 demo depends on it.

Open questions to surface as you scroll

  • Anyone whose project description in column 2 doesn't match what they remember saying — that's a flag for misalignment, not a typo. Talk it out.
  • Anyone whose pass/fail moment feels like it'd take longer than 25 min — we shrink the win, not the time.
  • Anyone whose check-in line feels patronizing or off — let them tell you the line they'd actually want to hear.
