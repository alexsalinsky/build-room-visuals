# 01 — Tone of Voice

How Alex actually writes. Pulled from his LinkedIn Newsletter editions, the Nomad Cruise affiliate copy, and the quotes he's spoken on camera. This is descriptive, not aspirational. Match this pattern.

---

## The 7 voice rules

### 1. Short sentences. Fragments are fine.
Cut every sentence that runs more than 18 words unless the rhythm needs the long one for contrast. Fragments work when they punch.

**Yes:** "Threads burn. Files remain."
**Yes:** "Most ambitious operators I know are lonelier than they let on. AI made it worse."
**No:** "It's important to recognize that in the current landscape, ambitious operators are often facing increasing levels of isolation, which has been exacerbated by recent advancements in AI technology."

### 2. Direct second person. Talk TO one reader.
Address "you" specifically. Don't write to "audiences" or "leaders" or "operators."

**Yes:** "How do you get your team to use AI, and use it well?"
**Yes:** "Your team has Claude. They have ChatGPT. They probably have Copilot in their IDE."
**No:** "Leaders are asking how to drive AI adoption across their teams."

### 3. Name the problem before the solution.
Open with the pain or the question, not the announcement.

**Yes opener:** "The workshop you sent them to didn't move the number. People nodded along and went back to their old workflows."
**No opener:** "We're excited to announce our July cohort kickoff."

### 4. Concrete > abstract. Numbers, names, specifics.
Real proof. Not vibes.

**Yes:** "About 28% are AI Novices, 69% Experimenters, 2.7% Practitioners, 0.08% Experts. Most of your team is in the middle."
**Yes:** "Opens with a $3M case study on inefficient meeting structures."
**No:** "Many teams struggle with AI adoption at scale."

### 5. No corporate filler. Anti-buzzword.
Kill on sight: leverage, synergies, unlock, ecosystem, journey (in the corporate sense), holistic, robust, scalable solution, drive value, mission-critical, best-in-class.

**Yes:** "Build something working."
**No:** "Unlock scalable AI capabilities."

### 6. Punchline sentences end paragraphs.
End on the thing that lands, then move on. Don't trail off.

**Yes:** "Sustained behavior change is the goal. Everything below is in service of that."
**Yes:** "A team with one quiet AI hero hasn't moved. A team where capability spreads has."
**No:** "...and so it's clear that there are many factors to consider when implementing these strategies."

### 7. Rule of three when listing.
Three pillars, three steps, three things. Brain hooks on threes.

**Yes:** "Three pillars: Define, Measure, Facilitate."
**Yes:** "Learn AI. Build Robots. Stay Human." (mission, verbatim, always)

---

## The HARD nos

These break the voice every time:

- **No em dashes.** Use commas, periods, or restructure. This is non-negotiable.
- **No "drop in chat" prompts** in any post or asset. "Comment below" + variants are out. (Operational mentions like "link is in the chat" inside a live session are fine.)
- **No fabricated callbacks.** Don't write "answering your question on the call" or "the X idea you mentioned" unless it actually happened.
- **No paraphrasing the mission.** "Learn AI. Build Robots. Stay Human." goes verbatim every time, or doesn't go at all.
- **No "we're excited to announce" / "thrilled to share" / "honored to."** Just say the thing.
- **No hedging at the start.** "I think maybe possibly" is out. State it.

## Voice fingerprint examples (Alex's published words)

Read these out loud once. Voice lives in rhythm, not vocabulary.

**Thesis lines:**
- "Learn AI. Build Robots. Stay Human."
- "Threads burn. Files remain."

**Problem-first opener (LinkedIn newsletter):**
> "How do you get your team to use AI, and use it well? It's not a tools question. Your team has Claude. They have ChatGPT. They probably have Copilot in their IDE and Gemini in their inbox. It's not a training question either. The workshop you sent them to didn't move the number."

**Mission-flavored hook:**
> "Most ambitious operators I know are lonelier than they let on. AI made it worse. The people who jumped first, who built the tools, who restructured their teams around agents, they're often the most isolated."

**Direct outcome promise:**
> "Everyone leaves the ship with a tool they made."

**Personal-stake story:**
> "I'm here in Medellín, Colombia, and the community around me, I feel like I'm losing some of my more ambitious friends to AI. 'Alex, I'm so revved up on AI. I've built so much. It's transformed my company, but I haven't been to the gym.'"

**Cohort promise (the BC/AC pattern):**
> "My hope is that in your mind there's BC and AC, before cruise and after cruise in your AI journey, and that a few months after the cruise you point back and you say, that was a turning point. I built something. I connected with people. And now my life is kind of changed for it."

## When you have to write something brand new and you're stuck

The fallback formula (use sparingly, it's a crutch):

```
[1-sentence problem statement, second person]
[1-sentence reframe: it's actually X]
[1-sentence what good looks like]
[1 line CTA: the next step is Y]
```

Example:
> Your team has the AI tools. They're not using them. The block isn't training, it's the gap between knowing and shipping. July 15 we run a Kickoff Session. You leave with one thing you'll build.
