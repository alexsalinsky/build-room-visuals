# 02 — Hard Rules (Cheat Sheet)

Print this. Pin it.

---

## Voice

- ✗ No em dashes. Ever.
- ✗ No "drop in chat" / "comment below" / audience-direction prompts.
- ✗ No "we're excited to announce" / "thrilled to share."
- ✗ No corporate filler: leverage, synergies, unlock, ecosystem, journey, holistic, robust, scalable, mission-critical, best-in-class.
- ✗ No fabricated callbacks ("answering your question on the call") unless it actually happened.
- ✓ Short sentences. Fragments are fine.
- ✓ Direct second person ("you," not "leaders" or "audiences").
- ✓ Problem first, then reframe, then next step.
- ✓ Numbers, names, dates over vibes.
- ✓ End paragraphs on a punchline.

## Mission line

- ✓ Verbatim every time: **"Learn AI. Build Robots. Stay Human."**
- ✗ Never paraphrased. Never reordered. Never partially quoted.

## Event names

- ✓ "Kickoff Session" — NOT "webinar," NOT "masterclass," NOT "workshop."
- ✓ "Cohort" — what we run (4-week, virtual).
- ✓ "Retreat" — 5-day in-person.
- ✓ "Open Cohort" for individuals. "Closed Cohort" for booked teams.

## Calls to action

- ✓ ONE CTA per post. Not two, not three.
- ✓ The payment link goes in the live Kickoff Session chat ONCE per session. No pressure close.
- ✗ No "limited spots! act now!" hype. Scarcity push is its own moment, late in the funnel.

## Cohort sales

- ✗ NEVER imply that cohort cap is sold out unless it actually is.
- ✗ NEVER promise specific outcomes you can't back up with proof from the [Proof Library](https://alexsalinsky.github.io/build-room-visuals/proof-library.html).
- ✓ Always check the Proof Library before quoting a testimonial or win.

## Posting cadence (default, can change per launch)

- IG: 2-4 posts per week during a launch window
- WhatsApp: 1 broadcast per launch milestone (first post, day-before Kickoff, scarcity push)
- LinkedIn: 1-2 posts per week, longer-form
- Skool: 1-2 posts per week into the community

## When in doubt

1. Send the draft to Alex.
2. Wait 10 minutes.
3. If you don't hear back, ship the draft. Replace later if needed. Speed beats polish.
