# 06 — Starter Copy Templates

Five drafts, in Alex's voice, ready to ship or adapt for a typical Kickoff Session launch. Swap dates + links and you've got a launch.

**Context for all five:**
- The event: a Kickoff Session (90 min, live on Zoom)
- The cohort: starts the week after Kickoff (TSLS = Tech Setup / Ledger Session)
- The CTA: one link, registers them for the Kickoff Session
- The payment link goes into the live Kickoff Session chat ONCE during the event, NOT in the promo posts

---

## A. Instagram post (square, 1:1)

### Caption (post text):

```
Your team has the AI tools.
They're not using them.

The block isn't training.
It's the gap between knowing and shipping.

[DATE]. We run a Kickoff Session.
You leave with one thing you'll build.

Link in bio.

Learn AI. Build Robots. Stay Human.
```

### Image guidance:
Warm, brand-aligned background with the date prominent and "Kickoff Session" as the headline. Bottom corner: "The Build Room" wordmark.

### Alt text:
"[Date] Kickoff Session announcement. The Build Room."

---

## B. WhatsApp broadcast (short, personal)

### Message text:

```
Quick one.

[DATE], I'm running a Kickoff Session for the next Build Room cohort.

90 min. Live. You'll leave with one thing you're going to build, and the next step to ship it.

If you want to come, the link is here: [REGISTRATION LINK]

If now isn't the right time, no problem. Pass it on to one person you think it's for.

Alex
```

### Notes:
- Length: under 50 words for the body. WhatsApp = brisk.
- One CTA. No "let me know if you have questions."
- The "pass it on" line is the soft referral mechanic.
- For 1:1 sends, personalize the opener ("Quick one — saw your post about X, made me think of this").

## C. LinkedIn post (longer, framework-flavored)

### Post text:

```
Most teams I talk to have the AI tools.

Claude. ChatGPT. Copilot. Probably a paid Notion AI seat someone never opened.

The tools aren't the problem. The training isn't either. The workshop you sent your team to didn't move the number. People nodded along and went back to their old workflows.

What's actually missing is a structured place to build.

Not a course. Not a Slack channel. Not another async tutorial.

A 4-week cohort where you ship one real thing, with peers building alongside you, and a facilitator pushing on the things that matter.

[DATE] we run the Kickoff Session for the next cohort.

90 minutes. Live. You leave with the one thing you're going to build, the people you'll build it with, and the first step you take this week.

If this is the season for you, the link to the Kickoff Session is here: [REGISTRATION LINK]

If it's not, forward this to the operator on your team who's been asking about AI but hasn't shipped yet.

Learn AI. Build Robots. Stay Human.
```

### Notes:
- Length: ~200 words. LinkedIn rewards 150-300 word posts.
- Opens with the problem in second person.
- Names the false solutions ("not a course, not a Slack channel").
- Names what good actually looks like.
- One CTA + one soft referral ask.
- Mission line as the closer (verbatim).
- No em dashes — check before posting.

### Image guidance:
A real photo of a past cohort moment, OR a clean text-on-background graphic with the headline "Your team has the AI tools. They're not using them."

## D. Skool community post

Audience here is warmer — existing community members, some past cohort alumni. Lower-pressure framing.

```
Heads up — Kickoff Session for the next cohort is [DATE].

90 min, live on Zoom. If you've been thinking about jumping into a cohort, this is the front door.

What's different about this round:
- Tighter pre-work (the Bot Setup Standards module ships you in with tools wired)
- Tech Setup / Ledger Session the week after (so late buyers can still join)
- Same 4-week build arc with the demo day close

Link to register: [REGISTRATION LINK]

If you're alumni and you know one person this is for, send them the link directly. That's the strongest signal we get.
```

### Notes:
- Names what's new since the last cohort.
- Warm-audience CTA: alumni referrals.
- Doesn't pitch the mission line — community already knows it.

## E. Email blast

### Subject:

```
The [Month] Kickoff Session
```

### Preheader:

```
90 minutes live. You leave with one thing you'll build.
```

### Body:

```
Quick one.

[DATE] I'm running the Kickoff Session for the next Build Room cohort.

Here's the shape:
- 90 min live on Zoom (or wherever you want to call in from)
- You leave with the one thing you'll build, the people you'll build it with, and the first step you take this week
- The cohort runs 4 weeks after that, demo day at the end

Who it's for: operators and founders who have the AI tools, haven't gotten leverage from them, and are tired of doing it alone.

Who it's not for: anyone looking for another tutorial. This is a build cohort, not a course.

Link to the Kickoff Session is here: [REGISTRATION LINK]

If now isn't the season, reply and tell me where you're at. I'll tell you straight whether this cohort is the right fit.

Alex

P.S. The payment link to join the cohort goes into the Kickoff Session chat ONCE during the live event. No pressure close. We make the offer once and you decide on your own time.
```

### Notes:
- Subject line: 5 words or less, no clickbait, no emoji.
- Preheader = the promise + outcome.
- "Who it's for / who it's not for" pattern.
- The P.S. signals the soft-sell discipline — that's a feature for the audience.

---

## Voice check before you ship

Run every draft past this 5-question check:

1. Are there any em dashes? (Should be zero.)
2. Did I open with a problem or with "we're excited"? (Should be problem.)
3. Is there ONE CTA? (Not two, not three.)
4. Did I use the mission line correctly? (Verbatim or not at all.)
5. Did I name a concrete outcome the reader can picture? (Not vibes.)

If all 5 are clean, ship it.
