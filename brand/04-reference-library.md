# 04 — Reference Library

If you read these things, you'll know how to write in Alex's voice without checking with him.

---

## Priority 1 — the 3 LinkedIn Newsletter editions

These are Alex's longest-form public writing. Read all three. Pay attention to: opener, paragraph length, where he uses bold, where he cites a number, how he ends paragraphs.

### "How to Get Your Team to Actually Use AI" (Jun 1, 2026)
- https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/how-get-your-team-actually-use-ai-alex-salinsky-rmmhe
- **Why this one first:** clearest framework piece. Opens with "How do you get your team to use AI, and use it well?" — perfect example of problem-first opener.
- **Notice:** the rule of three (Define, Measure, Facilitate), second-person address throughout, punchline closes.

### "AI and the Devil: How to Understand Our Part in The Existential Threat" (Apr 21, 2026)
- https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/ai-devil-how-understand-our-part-existential-threat-alex-salinsky-gwuee
- **Why:** vision / mission-flavored piece. Pairs with the "Stay Human" thread.
- **Notice:** social media algorithms as the cautionary frame for AI's societal impact. Concrete analogy, not abstraction.

### "Your Meetings Are Probably Costing You Millions" (Mar 23, 2026)
- https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/your-meetings-probably-costing-you-millions-alex-salinsky-fmxte
- **Why:** opens with a $3M case study. Perfect example of "concrete > abstract."
- **Notice:** operations/leadership angle, not AI directly. Same voice, different topic.

## Priority 3 — comparison writers (external)

Generic marketing blogs will pull you toward generic SaaS-marketing voice. Alex's voice is operator-direct, not SaaS-marketer. These external writers have similar DNA — useful comparison material:

- **David Perell's Write of Passage sales pages** (writeofpassage.school) — founder-led cohort copy
- **Alex Hormozi's $100M Offers PDF** (acquisition.com) — direct outcome-first writing
- **Daniel Priestley's Oversubscribed** (book) — demand-side framing
- **Wes Kao's essays** (weskao.com) — cohort-business-specific voice

## How to use this list

You don't need to read it all this week. Suggested:
1. This week: 1 LinkedIn newsletter edition
2. Next week: 2nd newsletter edition + skim proof library
3. Within the month: 3rd newsletter edition + 1-2 of the comparison writers

Track what lands. Note which patterns you want to lean into more.
